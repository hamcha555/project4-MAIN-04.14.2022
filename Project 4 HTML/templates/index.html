College is an exciting time for many young people. It’s common for many college students to experience stress because of the new challenges college brings — moving away from home, studying, and adjusting to a new life.


Trying to cope with so many new changes may cause some students to deal with depression. Studies show that more students are dealing with depression than those of previous generations. Explore this site to learn more about the signs of depression and what you can do to manage your mental health.


Symptoms of depression and anxiety can include:
  • Difficulty handling schoolwork
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in eating or sleeping patterns
  • Emotional outbursts, such as tearfulness or anger
  • Sense of being overwhelmed
  • Panic
  • Faulty self-assessments
  • Lack of energy

Don't Let Stigma Stop You From Getting Help
"We all have mental health, like we all have physical health. Both change throughout our lives. And, like our bodies, our minds can become unwell."

Managing your mental health is important. Actively participating in your healthcare and understanding what it means to take care of yourself is the first step of taking charge of your mental health. Learn more about managing your mental health and get tips on navigating school and relationships.

If you have a loved one who is struggling with mental illness, it is important to understand your role as a supportive figure in thier lives. It is also important to understand how their mental health impacts you. Learn more about the impacts of mental health and ways to take care of yourself and your loved ones.
