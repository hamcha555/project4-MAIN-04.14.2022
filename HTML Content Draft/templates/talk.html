Talk About It!

When it comes to mental illness it is important to know that you are not alone. Talking about mental health is a powerful first step towards impoving your wellbeing. Begin the conversation around your mental health and connect to others who are going through similar situations.

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline
1‑800‑273‑TALK (8255)

The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline is a national network of local crisis centers that provides free and confidential emotional support to people in suicidal crisis or emotional distress 24 hours a day, 7 days a week in the United States.









OK2Talk
National Alliance on Mental Illness

OK2TALK is a community where teens and young adults struggling with mental health conditions can find a safe place to talk about what they’re experiencing by sharing their personal stories of recovery, tragedy, struggle or hope. Anyone can add their voice by sharing stories, poems, inspirational quotes, photos, videos, song lyrics and messages of support in a safe, moderated space.









Active Minds
Changing the conversation about mental health

Active Minds is a national leader for young adult mental health advocacy/suicide prevention and brings to mental health what no other organization can — the voice of young people who are disproportionately affected by mental illnesses and the way mental health is addressed on campuses and in society at large.
Active Minds is present in more than 1,000 schools, communities and workplaces nationwide, including over 600 student-led chapters. Active Minds offers programs and services empower young adults to reduce the stigma surrounding mental health, create communities of support, and ultimately save lives.
